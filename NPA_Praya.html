Operations Division

Mission-Driven Policing

We balance swift response with community trust. From dispatch to on-scene command, our priority system ensures resources are assigned based on threat to life, vulnerability, and public order.

Response Priorities

  • Priority 1: Life-threatening incidents, violent crimes in progress, missing children.
  • Priority 2: Serious property crimes, active traffic hazards, urgent welfare checks.
  • Priority 3: Non-injury collisions, delayed reports, quality-of-life complaints.

Real-Time Command Center

District commanders monitor live incident queues, AVL vehicle locations, and drone-assisted views to deploy the closest available unit.

What to report online

Use eReport for thefts under ¤2,500, vandalism, lost property, identity fraud, and supplemental witness statements. Emergencies must be reported by dialing 911.

Traffic Safety

Uniformed traffic units conduct sobriety checkpoints, commercial vehicle inspections, and school zone enforcement. Collision reconstruction teams publish findings within 10 business days.

Specialized Support

Canine teams, crisis negotiators, and air support are on-call for high-risk warrants, missing persons searches, and mass-casualty coordination. Behavioral health co-responders accompany patrol officers on welfare checks.

Records & Licensing

Secure Records. Clear Standards.

Request certified documents, verify eligibility, and schedule licensing appointments without visiting a station.

Police Clearance Certificates

Certificates cover national criminal history, open warrants, and verification of identity. Average processing time is three business days.

  • Standard clearance for employment and visas
  • Vendor vetting for public contracts
  • Expedited service for court orders and humanitarian travel
  • Mobile fingerprinting teams for residents with limited mobility

Firearm Licensing

Applicants must complete accredited safety training, provide secure storage proof, and pass biometric background checks. Commercial licenses require annual compliance audits and proof of liability insurance.

Records Release Standards

All public records are reviewed for statutory redactions. Certified digital copies include tamper-evident seals and can be validated online by employers, insurers, and courts.

Traffic Records

Request certified collision reports, citation histories, and commercial vehicle inspection outcomes. Digital signatures are verifiable through the QR seal on each PDF.

Identity Verification
Protecting your data

Applicants must present a national ID or passport and two proofs of address. Digital submissions use one-time passcodes and face match checks to prevent impersonation.

Community Safety

Prevention Through Partnership

We invest in trust-based policing, youth mentorship, and data-informed prevention to reduce crime before it happens.

Programs

  • School safety officers delivering anti-bullying curriculum and safe passage patrols.
  • Neighborhood watch onboarding with rapid alert channels and patrol ride-alongs.
  • Victim services providing counseling, relocation support, and court accompaniment.
  • Business liaison program offering security surveys, alarm registration, and robbery prevention coaching.

Transparency

Quarterly dashboards publish use-of-force statistics, bias audit results, and complaint resolutions. Community advisory boards review policies, body-worn camera deployment plans, and surveillance technology proposals.

Careers

Join the National Police

Serve with integrity, empathy, and professionalism. The Academy trains officers, dispatchers, analysts, and civilian specialists.

Academy Pathways

  • Patrol officer — 24-week academy, crisis intervention, and defensive tactics.
  • Communications officer — emergency call-taking, triage, and stress management.
  • Digital forensics — cyber evidence handling, malware response, and courtroom testimony.

Eligibility

Candidates must be 21+, complete a background investigation, pass medical and fitness standards, and commit to the Code of Ethics.

Employee Support

Officers receive wellness counseling, tuition reimbursement, and quarterly resiliency training. Equipment stipends cover body armor, duty gear, and technology allowances.

Application windows
Updated weekly

Spring and autumn academy classes accept rolling applications. Lateral transfers receive credit for prior service and compressed field training.

Scholarships
Serve your community

Tuition assistance is available for applicants from underrepresented communities, veterans, and STEM graduates entering digital investigations.

About the Agency

Accountable, Transparent, Ready

Founded in 2010, the National Police Agency coordinates municipal and regional police bodies under one professional standard.

Leadership

Director-General Lía Cabrera leads the Agency with a focus on procedural justice, technology-driven policing, and officer wellness.

Inspectorate & Oversight

An independent Inspectorate audits use-of-force, evidence handling, procurement, and whistleblower protections. Annual reports and discipline summaries are published for public review.

Accountability

Every critical incident is reviewed by a civilian-majority board, with timelines for public release of body-worn camera footage and investigative findings.
