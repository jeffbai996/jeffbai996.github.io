Professional Licensing

Apply for, renew, or verify healthcare professional licenses

License Types

The Department of Health issues licenses for the following healthcare professions:

Physician License

For medical doctors (MD) and doctors of osteopathic medicine (DO).

P$850 / 2 years

Nursing License

For registered nurses (RN) and licensed practical nurses (LPN).

P$250 / 2 years

Pharmacist License

For registered pharmacists and pharmacy technicians.

P$400 / 2 years

Dental License

For dentists, dental hygienists, and dental assistants.

P$550 / 2 years

Allied Health License

Physical therapists, occupational therapists, and other allied professionals.

P$300 / 2 years

Mental Health License

Psychologists, counselors, and clinical social workers.

P$450 / 2 years

Application Requirements

All license applications require:

  • Completed application form
  • Proof of education (certified transcripts)
  • Passing scores on required examinations
  • Background check clearance
  • Proof of identity (PrayaID or passport)
  • Application fee payment

Renewal Deadline

All licenses expiring December 31, 2025 must be renewed by November 30, 2025 to avoid late fees and potential practice interruption.

Continuing Education Requirements

Licensed healthcare professionals must complete continuing education credits to maintain active licensure. Requirements vary by profession:

Profession CE Credits Required Cycle
Physicians 50 credits Per 2-year cycle
Nurses 30 credits Per 2-year cycle
Pharmacists 40 credits Per 2-year cycle
Dentists 40 credits Per 2-year cycle
Allied Health 24 credits Per 2-year cycle

Disease Surveillance

The Department of Health maintains active surveillance for notifiable diseases. Healthcare providers are required to report cases of:

  • Tuberculosis
  • HIV/AIDS
  • Hepatitis (A, B, C)
  • Measles
  • Dengue fever
  • Food-borne illness outbreaks
  • Any unusual disease clusters

Health Promotion Programs

Current public health initiatives include:

  • Healthy Praya 2030 – National health improvement strategy
  • Smoke-Free Praya – Tobacco cessation support
  • Move More Praya – Physical activity promotion
  • Mental Wellness Initiative – Community mental health resources

About the Department

Our mission, leadership, and organizational structure

Our Mission

The Department of Health is committed to protecting and promoting the health and well-being of all Praya citizens through evidence-based policies, quality healthcare regulation, and accessible public health services.

Vision

A healthy Praya where every citizen has access to quality healthcare and the opportunity to achieve their full health potential.

Core Functions

  • Healthcare Regulation – Licensing and oversight of healthcare facilities and professionals
  • Public Health – Disease prevention, immunization programs, and health promotion
  • Health Policy – Development of national health strategies and guidelines
  • Emergency Preparedness – Pandemic planning and health emergency response
  • Health Information – Collection and analysis of health statistics

Leadership

Secretary of Health Dr. Maria Chen, MD, MPH
Deputy Secretary Dr. James Wong, PhD
Chief Medical Officer Dr. Sarah Lim, MD
Director, Public Health Dr. Michael Torres, DrPH
Director, Healthcare Licensing Patricia Huang, JD

Divisions

  • Healthcare Facilities Licensing Division
  • Professional Licensing Division
  • Public Health Division
  • Epidemiology and Disease Control Division
  • Health Promotion Division
  • Emergency Preparedness Division
  • Health Information and Statistics Division
  • Pharmaceutical Services Division

Legal Authority

The Department of Health operates under the authority of the Public Health Act (2012), the Healthcare Facilities Licensing Act (2013), and the Health Professionals Regulation Act (2014). All regulations are promulgated in accordance with the Administrative Procedure Act.

File a Complaint

Report concerns about healthcare providers or facilities

Medical Emergency?

If you are experiencing a medical emergency, call 995 immediately. This form is not for emergency situations.

What You Can Report

  • Unprofessional conduct by healthcare providers
  • Unlicensed practice of medicine or healthcare
  • Unsafe conditions at healthcare facilities
  • Billing fraud or abuse
  • Violations of patient rights
  • Medication errors or pharmacy concerns

Submit a Complaint

Include dates, locations, and any witnesses if applicable
What Happens Next
Investigation process

1. Review
Your complaint is reviewed within 5 business days.

2. Investigation
If warranted, a formal investigation is opened.

3. Resolution
You will be notified of the outcome.

Most investigations are completed within 90 days.

Confidentiality

Your identity is kept confidential to the extent permitted by law. Anonymous complaints are accepted but may limit investigation options.
