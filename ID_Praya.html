Land Registry Division

Secure, transparent property registration and title management

The Land Registry Division maintains the official register of all land parcels within the Republic of Praya. Our mandate is to provide a secure, efficient, and transparent system for the registration and transfer of property rights.

PRISM â€” Property Registry Information System

PRISM is Praya's digital land registry platform, providing online access to title searches, property records, and registration services. The system guarantees the integrity of property records through blockchain-backed verification.

Guaranteed Titles

All registered titles in Praya are guaranteed by the Government under the Land Registration Act (R.S. 4 Â§ LR-2201). This means that registered owners are protected against loss arising from defects in title.

Services

ðŸ” Title Search

Search the PRISM database for land titles, ownership history, and encumbrances. Basic searches are free; certified copies require a fee.

ðŸ“ Property Registration

Register new property acquisitions, transfers, subdivisions, and consolidations. All instruments affecting land must be registered to be effective against third parties.

ðŸ“œ Certified Copies

Obtain certified copies of title certificates, survey plans, and historical records for legal and financial purposes.

ðŸ—ï¸ Subdivision & Consolidation

Apply to subdivide a parcel into multiple lots or consolidate adjacent parcels into a single title.

Registration Process

1

Submit Application

Complete Form LR-101 and submit with required documents through PRISM or at any Interior Department service centre.

2

Document Verification

Our registrars verify all documents, check for encumbrances, and ensure compliance with planning requirements.

3

Survey Confirmation

For new parcels or boundary changes, a licensed surveyor must submit a survey plan to the Cadastral Survey Office.

4

Registration Complete

Once approved, the new title is entered into PRISM and you receive your Certificate of Title.

Required Documents

  • Completed application form (LR-101 for transfers, LR-102 for new registrations)
  • Original or certified copy of the instrument (deed, mortgage, lease, etc.)
  • Praya Identity Card (PIC) or passport of all parties
  • Survey plan (where applicable)
  • Planning approval certificate (for subdivisions)
  • Payment of registration fees and stamp duty

Important Notice

All property transfers must be registered within 30 days of execution. Late registration may incur penalties under the Registration of Instruments Act.

Building Permits & Construction

Ensuring safe and sustainable development across Praya

The Building Control Division administers the Building Control Act and its regulations, ensuring that all construction in Praya meets safety, structural, and sustainability standards.

PermitConnect â€” Online Permit Portal

PermitConnect is the Interior Department's one-stop platform for building permit applications. Submit applications, track status, schedule inspections, and receive approvals electronically.

Types of Permits

ðŸ Development Permit (Form BP-100)

Required for new buildings, extensions, and major alterations. Includes review of architectural plans, structural engineering, and environmental impact.

ðŸ”§ Minor Works Permit (Form BP-110)

For smaller projects such as interior renovations, fencing, and minor structural changes not affecting building safety.

ðŸ—ï¸ Demolition Permit (Form BP-120)

Required before demolishing any structure. Includes hazardous materials assessment and waste disposal plan.

ðŸ“‹ Temporary Structure Permit (Form BP-130)

For temporary buildings, scaffolding, and event structures. Valid for up to 12 months with possible extension.

âš¡ Electrical Installation Permit (Form BP-140)

Required for new electrical systems and major modifications. Must be certified by a licensed electrical contractor.

Fast-Track Processing

Simple residential projects (single-family homes, standard extensions) are eligible for fast-track processing with approval within 10 business days.

Application Process

1

Pre-Application Consultation (Optional)

Schedule a consultation to discuss your project and identify requirements before formal submission.

2

Submit Application

Complete the appropriate form and upload all required documents through PermitConnect.

3

Plan Review

Building inspectors review your plans for compliance with building codes and zoning regulations.

4

Permit Issued

Upon approval, your permit is issued electronically. Display the permit prominently at the construction site.

5

Inspections

Schedule mandatory inspections at key milestones (foundation, framing, final).

6

Certificate of Completion

After final inspection approval, receive your Certificate of Completion (Form BP-900).

Required Documents

  • Completed application form
  • Site plan showing property boundaries and proposed construction
  • Architectural drawings (floor plans, elevations, sections)
  • Structural engineering drawings and calculations
  • Proof of land ownership or owner's consent
  • Environmental impact assessment (for large projects)
  • Fire safety plan

Building Without a Permit

Unauthorized construction is a serious offense under the Building Control Act. Penalties include fines up to $50,000, mandatory demolition, and possible imprisonment for repeat offenders.

Civil Registry Division

Recording life's most important moments

The Civil Registry Division maintains official records of vital events for all Praya citizens and residents. These records establish legal identity and are essential for accessing government services, education, healthcare, and employment.

Vital Records

ðŸ‘¶ Birth Registration

All births in Praya must be registered within 42 days. Birth certificates are issued at no cost for the first certified copy. Parents or guardians are required to register the birth at any Civil Registry office or hospital registration desk.

ðŸ’ Marriage Registration

Couples intending to marry must file a Notice of Intended Marriage at least 21 days before the ceremony. Marriage certificates are issued after the solemnization by a registered Marriage Officiant.

âš±ï¸ Death Registration

Deaths must be registered within 5 days. Registration is typically done by the hospital, nursing home, or funeral director. Family members may apply for certified copies of death certificates.

ðŸ“ Name Changes

Praya citizens may apply to change their legal name through the Deed Poll process. Applications are reviewed for legitimacy and published in the Government Gazette before taking effect.

Digital Certificates

Since 2023, all vital records certificates include a QR code for instant verification. Digital certificates can be stored in the SingularID mobile app for convenient access.

Parks & Natural Resources Division

Preserving Praya's natural heritage for future generations

The Parks & Natural Resources Division manages Praya's system of national parks, nature reserves, and protected areas. Our mission is to conserve biodiversity, protect natural landscapes, and provide opportunities for recreation and environmental education.

Protected Area System

Praya's protected area network encompasses over 142,000 square kilometres, representing approximately 18% of the nation's land area. The system includes:

  • 12 National Parks: Large natural areas of national significance
  • 23 Nature Reserves: Areas protecting specific ecosystems or species
  • 8 Marine Protected Areas: Coastal and oceanic conservation zones
  • 4 Wilderness Areas: Remote, undeveloped natural areas

Featured National Parks

A UNESCO World Heritage Site protecting one of the world's most diverse coral reef ecosystems. Over 500 species of fish and 200 species of coral.

Ancient redwood forests with trees over 1,000 years old. Popular for hiking, camping, and nature photography.

Park Entry Fees

Most national parks require an entry fee. Praya citizens and permanent residents receive discounted rates. Annual passes offer unlimited access to all parks.

About the Interior Department

Building the foundation of our nation since 2012

Our Mission

The Interior Department serves as the steward of Praya's physical and civic infrastructure. We are responsible for land administration, building regulation, civil registration, and the conservation of our natural heritage. Our mission is to provide secure, efficient, and accessible services that support the development of our nation while preserving it for future generations.

History

The Interior Department was established upon the founding of the Republic of Praya on July 1, 2012. Combining functions previously scattered across multiple colonial-era bureaus, the Department was created to provide unified administration of domestic affairs.

Key milestones include the launch of the PRISM digital land registry in 2018, the designation of the first national parks in 2014, and the modernization of the Civil Registry with digital certificates in 2023.

Leadership

Secretary of the Interior

The Hon. David K. Reyes, MP
Appointed: March 2023
Secretary Reyes previously served as Deputy Secretary and has over 20 years of experience in public administration.

Organizational Structure

The Department comprises five principal divisions:

  • Land Registry Division: Property registration, title management, cadastral surveys
  • Building Control Division: Construction permits, inspections, building codes
  • Civil Registry Division: Vital records (births, marriages, deaths), name changes
  • Parks & Natural Resources Division: National parks, nature reserves, conservation
  • Fire Services Division: Fire prevention, emergency response, safety inspections

Strategic Priorities 2025â€“2030

  • Digital Transformation: Complete digitization of all paper records and expansion of online services
  • Sustainable Development: Integration of environmental considerations into all planning and permitting decisions
  • Protected Area Expansion: Achieve 25% of national territory under protection by 2030
  • Service Excellence: Reduce average processing times by 30% across all services
  • Climate Resilience: Update building codes for climate adaptation
