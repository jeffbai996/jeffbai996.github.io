Eligibility Requirements

Who qualifies for public housing

To qualify for Housing Authority assistance, applicants must meet all of the following criteria:

Residency Requirements

  • Prayan Citizenship: Must be a citizen of the Republic of Praya with valid land rights
  • Active Presence: Minimum 3-day active presence in Praya required at allocation
  • No Conditional Stays: Cannot be subject to conditional stay restrictions

Property Ownership

  • Cannot currently own domestic property in Praya
  • No pending purchase agreements for property
  • Cannot hold more than 50% shares in property-owning companies
  • Former subsidized home ownership participants may qualify after agreement cancellation

Income & Assets

  • Monthly income must fall below established thresholds (see document HD273-A)
  • Net assets subject to specific limits based on household size
  • Financial verification required during application process

Criminal History

  • 2-month ineligibility period following most criminal sentencing
  • 10-month ineligibility period for vandalism-related offenses
  • Specific offenses may result in permanent ineligibility

Download Eligibility Guidelines

For complete income and asset thresholds, download document HD273-A: Public Housing Eligibility Standards from our forms section.

Tenant Resources

Support and information for residents

Rights and Responsibilities

As a public housing tenant, you have specific rights and responsibilities under the Prayan Housing Act (2012):

Tenant Rights

  • Safe and Habitable Housing: Units must meet minimum health and safety standards
  • Privacy: 48-hour advance notice required for non-emergency inspections
  • Fair Treatment: Protection from discrimination based on race, religion, or family status
  • Reasonable Accommodations: Modifications for tenants with disabilities
  • Grievance Process: Right to appeal housing authority decisions

Tenant Responsibilities

  • Pay rent on time (by the 5th of each month)
  • Maintain unit in clean and sanitary condition
  • Report maintenance issues promptly
  • Comply with lease terms and house rules
  • Notify authority of changes in household composition or income

Maintenance Requests

Submit maintenance requests through the tenant portal or by calling 1-800-HOUSING. Emergency repairs (no heat, water leaks, electrical hazards) are addressed within 24 hours. Non-emergency requests are typically completed within 5-7 business days.

Rent Calculation

Public housing rent is set at 25% of adjusted monthly income, recalculated annually based on income verification. Residents must report changes in employment or household income within 30 days.

Tenant Assistance Hotline

Need help or have questions? Call our dedicated support line at 1-800-HOUSING (1-800-468-7464) or visit your local housing office during business hours.
