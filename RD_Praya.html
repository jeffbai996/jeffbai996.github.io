Tax Information

Understanding your tax obligations in the Republic of Praya

Personal Income Tax

The Republic of Praya uses a progressive income tax system. Tax rates range from 0% to 35% based on your annual income:

  • ₱0 - ₱50,000: 0% (Tax-free threshold)
  • ₱50,001 - ₱150,000: 15%
  • ₱150,001 - ₱300,000: 20%
  • ₱300,001 - ₱500,000: 25%
  • ₱500,001 - ₱1,000,000: 30%
  • Above ₱1,000,000: 35%

Corporate Tax

Businesses operating in Praya are subject to corporate income tax on their profits. The standard corporate tax rate is 21% for most businesses. Small businesses with annual revenue under ₱250,000 qualify for a reduced rate of 15%.

Value Added Tax (VAT)

A 12% VAT applies to most goods and services sold in Praya. Certain essential items including basic food staples, medical supplies, and educational materials are VAT-exempt.

Important Deadlines

Annual tax returns are due by April 15 each year. Quarterly estimated tax payments are due on the 15th of April, July, October, and January.

Tax Credits & Deductions

Eligible taxpayers can claim various deductions and credits including:

  • Dependent deductions (₱15,000 per dependent)
  • Education expenses (up to ₱25,000 annually)
  • Medical expenses (up to ₱30,000 annually)
  • Retirement savings contributions (up to ₱50,000 annually)
  • Charitable donations (up to 20% of taxable income)

Penalty Information

Late filing incurs a penalty of 5% of unpaid tax per month, up to a maximum of 25%. Interest on unpaid taxes accrues at 1.5% per month.

File & Pay Taxes

Submit your tax returns and make payments online

Online Tax Portal

The Revenue Department's online portal allows you to file your tax returns, make payments, and manage your tax account 24/7. To get started, you'll need your Tax Identification Number (TIN) and a secure password.

First Time User?

Register for an account at revenue.gov.py/register using your TIN and government-issued ID. Activation takes 1-2 business days.

Filing Your Return

Follow these steps to file your tax return online:

  • Log in to the tax portal with your TIN and password
  • Select the appropriate tax form (Form 1040 for individuals, Form 1120 for corporations)
  • Enter your income, deductions, and credits information
  • Review your calculated tax liability or refund
  • Submit electronically and receive instant confirmation

Payment Options

The Revenue Department accepts the following payment methods:

  • Direct Debit: Authorize automatic withdrawal from your bank account
  • Credit/Debit Card: Pay securely online (2.5% convenience fee applies)
  • Bank Transfer: Transfer funds to Revenue Department account #RD-2025-TAX
  • In-Person: Visit any Revenue Department office or authorized bank

Payment Deadline

All tax payments are due by April 15. Payments received after this date will incur penalties and interest charges.

Installment Plans

If you owe more than ₱10,000 in taxes, you may be eligible for an installment payment plan. Contact our office at (555) 0123-TAX to discuss payment arrangements.

Business Tax Services

Registration, compliance, and resources for Praya businesses

Business Registration

All businesses operating in the Republic of Praya must register with the Revenue Department to obtain a Tax Identification Number (TIN). This applies to sole proprietorships, partnerships, corporations, and non-profit organizations.

Required Documents

  • Completed Business Registration Form (Form BR-100)
  • Proof of business address (lease agreement or property deed)
  • Owner/director identification documents
  • Business license from local municipality
  • Articles of incorporation (for corporations)

Registration Fee

The one-time business registration fee is ₱5,000 for most businesses. Small businesses with projected annual revenue under ₱100,000 pay a reduced fee of ₱2,000.

Ongoing Compliance

Registered businesses must fulfill the following obligations:

  • File quarterly estimated tax payments by the 15th of April, July, October, and January
  • Submit annual corporate tax return by April 15
  • Remit employee withholding taxes monthly
  • File monthly VAT returns if applicable
  • Maintain accurate financial records for at least 7 years

Tax Incentives

The Revenue Department administers several tax incentive programs:

  • Small Business Rate: 15% tax rate for businesses with revenue under ₱250,000
  • R&D Tax Credit: 20% credit for qualified research and development expenses
  • Green Business Deduction: Enhanced deductions for renewable energy investments
  • Export Incentive: Reduced rate of 18% on export revenues

Audit Information

Businesses may be selected for audit. If selected, you will receive written notice at least 30 days in advance. Maintain organized records to facilitate the audit process.

Tax Refunds

Track and manage your tax refund

Refund Processing

If you overpaid your taxes or are eligible for refundable tax credits, the Revenue Department will issue a refund. Most refunds are processed within 12-21 days of filing your return.

Check Refund Status

To check the status of your refund, you'll need:

  • Your Tax Identification Number (TIN)
  • The tax year of your return
  • The exact refund amount shown on your return

Where's My Refund?

Use our online refund tracker at revenue.gov.py/refund or call our automated refund hotline at (555) 0123-REF for 24/7 status updates.

Refund Payment Methods

Choose how you'd like to receive your refund:

  • Direct Deposit: Fastest option - funds arrive in 7-14 days. Provide your bank account and routing numbers on your tax return.
  • Paper Check: Mailed to the address on your return. Delivery takes 3-4 weeks from processing date.
  • Prepaid Debit Card: Available for taxpayers without bank accounts. Card arrives by mail in 2-3 weeks.

Refund Delays

Your refund may be delayed if:

  • Your return contains errors or missing information
  • You're claiming certain tax credits that require additional verification
  • Your return has been selected for review
  • You owe back taxes or other government debts

Refund Offset

Your refund may be reduced or offset to pay outstanding tax debts, child support obligations, or other government debts. You will receive written notice if this occurs.

Amending Your Return

If you discover an error after filing that would increase your refund, you can file an amended return using Form 1040-X within 3 years of the original filing date.
