Financial Markets

Trade equities, bonds, derivatives, and explore market indices on the PSE platform.

Equities Market
Main board and growth stocks

The PSE Equities Market is home to 342 listed companies across nine sectors, with a combined market capitalization of ¤2.8 trillion. Our Main Board hosts established corporations meeting stringent financial and governance standards, while the Growth Board provides smaller companies access to capital markets.

Trading Hours

  • Pre-Market Session: 08:30 - 09:30 (order entry and modification only)
  • Opening Auction: 09:30 (price discovery via order matching)
  • Continuous Trading: 09:30 - 15:45
  • Closing Auction: 15:45 - 16:00
  • Post-Market Session: 16:00 - 17:00 (for large block trades)

Market Structure

The PSE operates an order-driven electronic market using a central limit order book (CLOB). Orders are matched using price-time priority, ensuring fairness and transparency. All trades are executed through the PSE's proprietary trading engine with latency under 100 microseconds.

Key Statistics (as of Dec 2024)

Listed Companies: 342 (Main Board: 287 | Growth Board: 55)

Average Daily Value: ¤8.4 billion

Average Daily Volume: 124.5 million shares

Market Maker Program: 18 registered market makers

Listing Requirements

Main Board: Minimum paid-in capital of ¤500 million, three years of audited financials showing profitability, public float of at least 25%, and compliance with corporate governance codes.

Growth Board: Minimum capital of ¤100 million, two years of operations, public float of 20%, and appointment of a sponsor to guide compliance.

Bonds & Fixed Income
Government and corporate debt securities

The PSE Fixed Income platform facilitates trading of government bonds, municipal bonds, corporate bonds, and convertible securities. With over ¤650 billion in outstanding issuance, Praya's bond market provides investors with diverse income opportunities and issuers with flexible financing options.

Product Categories

  • Government Bonds (PGBs): Sovereign debt issued by the Republic of Praya in tenors from 2 to 30 years, denominated in Praya Dollar(s)(s). Outstanding volume: ¤420 billion.
  • Municipal Bonds: Debt securities issued by provincial and city governments for infrastructure projects. Tax-exempt for domestic investors.
  • Corporate Bonds: Investment-grade and high-yield bonds issued by PSE-listed and non-listed corporations. Minimum rating of B- required for listing.
  • Convertible Bonds: Hybrid securities convertible to equity at predetermined ratios, offering upside participation with downside protection.
  • Green Bonds: Dedicated financing for environmental and sustainability projects, certified under PSE Green Bond Framework.

Trading Mechanism

Bonds trade on a quote-driven market with registered dealers providing two-way pricing. Retail investors can access bonds through their brokers, with minimum transaction size of ¤50,000. Institutional block trades (¤10 million+) can be reported to the exchange after bilateral negotiation.

Derivatives Market
Options, futures, and structured products

The PSE Derivatives Exchange (PSEDX) offers a comprehensive suite of derivative instruments for hedging, speculation, and portfolio management. Launched in 2018, PSEDX has grown rapidly to handle over 400,000 contracts daily across equity derivatives, index futures, and commodity futures.

Equity Options

Call and put options are available on 87 highly liquid stocks, with standardized strike prices and monthly expiries. Contract size: 100 shares per contract. American-style exercise. Options are cash-settled T+1.

  • Trading Hours: 09:30 - 16:00 (aligned with spot market)
  • Tick Size: ¤0.05 for premiums under ¤10, ¤0.10 above
  • Margin Requirements: Calculated using industry-standard SPAN methodology
  • Market Makers: 8 registered options market makers providing continuous quotes

Index Futures

Futures contracts on PSE 30 Index, PSE All Share Index, and PSE Growth 50 Index. Contract multiplier: ¤50 per index point. Quarterly expiries (March, June, September, December).

Example: PSE 30 futures at 4,287 = contract value of ¤214,350. Initial margin: ¤21,435 (10%). Maintenance margin: ¤17,148 (8%).

Single Stock Futures (SSFs)

Leveraged exposure to individual stocks without options premium decay. Available on 45 large-cap stocks. One-month, two-month, and three-month contracts. Physical delivery or cash settlement at expiry.

Commodity Futures

The PSE has established contracts for key commodities important to Praya's economy:

  • Agricultural: Rice, coffee, sugar (physically settled at certified warehouses)
  • Energy: Crude oil, natural gas (cash-settled against international benchmarks)
  • Metals: Gold, silver, copper (cash-settled)

Structured Products

The PSE facilitates issuance and trading of structured products including:

  • Callable Bull/Bear Contracts (CBBCs): Leveraged instruments with mandatory call features for intraday trading
  • Warrants: Long-dated call and put warrants on indices and single stocks, issued by investment banks
  • Equity-Linked Notes (ELNs): Principal-protected or yield-enhancement structures combining bonds with embedded derivatives

Risk Disclosure

Derivatives trading involves substantial risk and may not be suitable for all investors. Leverage can amplify both gains and losses. Investors should fully understand the characteristics and risks of each product before trading. The PSE requires all derivative traders to complete an approved education course and sign risk disclosure statements.

Clearing & Settlement

All derivative trades are cleared through PSE Clearing Corporation (PSECC), a central counterparty (CCP) that becomes the buyer to every seller and seller to every buyer. PSECC maintains a default fund capitalized at ¤2.8 billion and employs real-time risk management to monitor participant exposures. Mark-to-market settlements occur daily, with variation margin called multiple times per day during volatile periods.

Services

Company Listing Services
Go public on the PSE

Initial Public Offerings (IPOs)

The PSE provides a transparent, efficient pathway for companies to access public capital markets. Our experienced listing team guides issuers through every stage from pre-application consultation to post-listing compliance.

Listing Process

  • Pre-Consultation (Month 0): Initial meeting with PSE Listing Division to assess eligibility, discuss board selection (Main vs Growth), and review requirements.
  • Formal Application (Month 1-2): Submit application form, audited financials, corporate governance documents, business plan, and use of proceeds. Application fee: ¤100,000.
  • Due Diligence (Month 3-4): PSE reviews all submitted documents, conducts management interviews, and may perform site visits. Applicant must appoint issue manager (investment bank) and legal counsel.
  • Approval (Month 5): PSE Listing Committee meets monthly to review applications. Conditional approval granted subject to satisfying outstanding items.
  • Offering Period (Month 6-7): Company files prospectus with Securities Commission, conducts roadshow, and opens IPO subscription. Book-building or fixed-price methods supported.
  • Trading Commencement (Month 8): Shares allocated to investors, listing ceremony held, trading begins on designated date.

Listing Fees

Initial Listing Fee: 0.02% of market capitalization (minimum ¤100,000, maximum ¤2,000,000)

Annual Listing Fee: Based on market cap tier, ranging from ¤50,000 to ¤500,000

Corporate Action Fees: Rights issues, stock splits, consolidations charged separately

Secondary Offerings

Already-listed companies can raise additional capital through:

  • Follow-on Offerings (FPOs): Sale of additional shares to public investors
  • Rights Issues: Offer of new shares to existing shareholders at discounted price
  • Private Placements: Sale of shares to select institutional investors (maximum 10% of capital in any 12-month period)

Alternative Listing Methods

  • Reverse Takeover (RTO): Private company acquires control of listed shell company
  • Direct Listing: Existing shares made tradeable without capital raise (subject to liquidity requirements)
  • SPAC Mergers: PSE permits Special Purpose Acquisition Companies; de-SPAC transactions must meet listing standards
Trading Membership
Become a PSE trading participant

Membership Categories

  • Trading Member: Full access to PSE trading platform for equities and bonds. Can execute proprietary and client orders. Minimum capital: ¤50 million.
  • Trading Clearing Member: Trading privileges plus direct clearing through PSECC. Can offer DMA to clients. Minimum capital: ¤150 million.
  • Derivatives Trading Participant: Authorization to trade PSEDX futures and options. Additional capital requirements and risk management standards apply.
  • Remote Member: Foreign brokers accessing PSE from overseas (subject to reciprocity agreements with home jurisdiction).

Application Process

Applicants must demonstrate adequate capital, qualified personnel (licensed traders and compliance officers), robust technology infrastructure, and comprehensive risk management frameworks. Background checks performed on all controlling shareholders and key executives.

Membership Fees

Application Fee: ¤200,000 (non-refundable) | Admission Fee: ¤1,000,000 (one-time) | Annual Membership Fee: ¤300,000 | Transaction Levy: 0.0050% of trade value

Technology Requirements

Members must connect to PSE via certified trading terminals supporting FIX 4.4 protocol. Co-location services available at PSE data center for low-latency trading. Disaster recovery and business continuity plans mandatory.

Trading Services
Advanced trading solutions

Order Types

  • Market Orders: Execute immediately at best available price
  • Limit Orders: Execute only at specified price or better
  • Stop Orders: Trigger market/limit order when stop price reached
  • Iceberg Orders: Display only portion of order size to reduce market impact
  • VWAP Orders: Execute throughout day targeting volume-weighted average price
  • MOC/LOC: Market/Limit on Close orders executed in closing auction

Algorithmic Trading

The PSE supports algorithmic trading with sub-millisecond latencies. Members can deploy DMA (Direct Market Access) connections and sponsor client algorithms subject to risk controls. All algorithms must be registered with PSE and comply with market conduct rules prohibiting manipulative strategies.

Block Trading Facility

Large institutional trades (minimum ¤10 million per transaction) can be negotiated privately and reported to PSE within 10 minutes. Block trades executed at prices within daily price limits receive automatic approval.

Clearing & Settlement
Post-trade infrastructure

PSE Clearing Corporation (PSECC)

PSECC operates as a central counterparty (CCP) for all PSE trades, guaranteeing settlement and managing counterparty risk. By novating trades, PSECC becomes the buyer to every seller and the seller to every buyer, eliminating bilateral credit risk.

Settlement Cycle

  • Equities: T+2 settlement (trade date + 2 business days)
  • Bonds: T+1 settlement
  • Derivatives: Daily mark-to-market with T+1 final settlement at expiry

Delivery vs Payment (DVP)

All settlements occur through DVP mechanism coordinated with Bank of Praya's RTGS system and PSE Depository. Securities transfer and cash payment happen simultaneously, eliminating principal risk.

Risk Management

PSECC employs multiple safeguards:

  • Margin Requirements: Initial and variation margin collected from clearing members
  • Default Fund: ¤2.8 billion mutualized fund covering clearing member defaults
  • Real-Time Monitoring: Continuous surveillance of participant exposures and credit usage
  • Stress Testing: Daily VaR calculations and weekly stress scenarios

Central Securities Depository (CSD)

PSE Depository maintains electronic book-entry records for all listed securities. Physical certificates eliminated; all holdings exist as dematerialized entries. Investors access holdings through CDS accounts at their brokers.

About PSE

Praya's premier securities exchange, connecting capital with opportunity since 1998.

Our History
Building Praya's financial future

Foundation (1998)

The Praya Stock Exchange was established following enactment of the Securities Market Act of 1997. The PSE began operations on March 2, 1998, with 42 founding listed companies and 12 trading members. Initial trading was conducted via open outcry on the exchange floor.

Electronic Trading Era (2003)

PSE migrated to fully electronic trading, retiring the physical trading floor. This modernization dramatically increased efficiency, reduced costs, and attracted international investors. Daily trading volume increased 400% within two years.

Derivatives Launch (2018)

The PSE Derivatives Exchange (PSEDX) commenced operations, offering equity options and index futures. This milestone provided Prayan investors with sophisticated hedging and speculation tools, further deepening capital markets.

Recent Developments (2020-Present)

The PSE has invested heavily in technology infrastructure, implementing sub-millisecond trading engines, expanding data center capacity, and launching mobile trading applications. Strategic initiatives include promoting ESG-compliant listings, developing a carbon credits trading platform, and exploring blockchain applications for post-trade settlement.

Governance
Leadership and oversight

Ownership Structure

The PSE is a demutualized, for-profit corporation owned by a consortium of institutional investors including banks, insurance companies, and asset managers. PSE shares are listed on its own exchange under ticker symbol "EXCH."

Board of Directors

The PSE Board consists of 11 members: 4 independent directors, 3 shareholder representatives, 2 trading member representatives, 1 listed company representative, and the CEO. The Board meets monthly and oversees strategy, risk management, and regulatory compliance.

Management Team

  • Chief Executive Officer: Michael Chen (appointed 2020, formerly CFO of Singapore Exchange)
  • Chief Operating Officer: Sarah Nakamura (oversees trading operations, technology, and market surveillance)
  • Chief Regulatory Officer: Dr. James Patel (supervises listing compliance, market conduct, and member regulation)
  • Chief Financial Officer: Maria Rodriguez (finance, treasury, and investor relations)

Regulatory Oversight

The PSE operates under supervision of the Praya Securities Commission (PSC), an independent statutory body. The PSC approves PSE rulebook amendments, reviews market conduct investigations, and ensures the exchange acts in the public interest.

Rules & Regulations
Market conduct standards

PSE Rulebook

The comprehensive PSE Rulebook governs all aspects of exchange operations including:

  • Listing Rules: Eligibility criteria, ongoing obligations, disclosure requirements, and delisting procedures
  • Trading Rules: Permitted order types, price limits, trading halts, error trade protocols, and prohibited conduct
  • Membership Rules: Capital requirements, personnel qualifications, technology standards, and disciplinary procedures
  • Clearing Rules: Settlement obligations, margin requirements, default management, and delivery procedures

Market Surveillance

The PSE Market Surveillance Division employs advanced algorithms to monitor trading in real-time, detecting potential market manipulation, insider trading, and other misconduct. Suspicious activities are investigated and referred to the PSC for enforcement action if warranted.

Prohibited Activities

  • Market manipulation (pump-and-dump schemes, spoofing, layering, wash trades)
  • Insider trading based on material non-public information
  • Front-running client orders
  • Spreading false rumors to influence prices
  • Cornering or squeezing the market

Violations may result in trading suspensions, fines up to ¤10 million, license revocation, and criminal prosecution.

Whistleblower Program

The PSE operates a confidential whistleblower hotline for reporting suspected market misconduct. Informants who provide original information leading to successful enforcement actions may receive awards of 10-30% of monetary sanctions collected, subject to maximum of ¤5 million.
