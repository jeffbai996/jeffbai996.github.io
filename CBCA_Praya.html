Customs & Trade

Tariffs, Trade & Compliance

Our harmonized tariff schedule ensures predictable duty collection on all commercial imports based on transparent CIF valuations.

Customs Declaration Process

All commercial importers must file electronic declarations through our Customs Portal before goods arrive at any entry point. Declarations require:

  • Complete description of goods and HS classification codes
  • Commercial invoice showing CIF value (Cost, Insurance, Freight)
  • Bill of lading or air waybill
  • Certificates of origin for preferential tariff treatment

Tariff Calculation

Duties are calculated based on the CIF value using rates from the Customs and Tariff Act (2018). The harmonized schedule covers over 5,000 product classifications with rates ranging from 0% to 30%.

De Minimis Threshold

Personal imports valued under ¤200 Praya Dollars are exempt from duty and do not require formal declarations. This exemption applies to mail shipments and accompanied baggage.

Tariff Relief Programs

Eligible importers can request exemptions or duty drawbacks for:

  • Goods for re-export or temporary admission
  • Humanitarian and disaster relief supplies
  • Educational materials for accredited institutions
  • Raw materials for domestic manufacturing (duty drawback)

Compliance & Audits

The CBCA conducts post-clearance audits to verify declared values, classifications, and origin claims. Penalties for misclassification, undervaluation, or fraud range from fines to criminal prosecution.

Immigration Services

Visas, Citizenship & Status

We facilitate the lawful entry and residence of foreign nationals while protecting national security and public health.

Visa Categories

  • Tourist Visa (T): Up to 90 days for leisure travel. Requires proof of onward travel and sufficient funds.
  • Business Visa (B): For meetings, conferences, and contract negotiations. Cannot be used for employment.
  • Work Visa (W): Requires employer sponsorship and labor market certification. Valid for 1-3 years, renewable.
  • Student Visa (S): Full-time enrollment at accredited institutions. Part-time work allowed up to 20 hours/week.
  • Family Reunification (F): For spouses, children, and dependent parents of citizens or permanent residents.

Permanent Residence

Foreign nationals may apply for permanent residence after 5 years of continuous lawful residence on a work visa, or 3 years if married to a Prayan citizen. Requirements include:

  • Clean criminal record and tax compliance
  • Basic Prayan language proficiency (B1 level)
  • Proof of stable income or employment
  • Medical examination and health insurance

Citizenship & Naturalization

Permanent residents may apply for citizenship after 5 years. The naturalization process includes a civics test, oath of allegiance, and background security checks.

Visa-Free Entry

Citizens of 42 countries can enter Praya visa-free for stays up to 90 days. Check the visa-free country list on our portal.

Border Enforcement

Preventing Smuggling & Fraud

Our enforcement division combats smuggling, counterfeit goods, and customs fraud through intelligence-led operations and technology.

Enforcement Priorities

  • Narcotics and controlled substances
  • Weapons, ammunition, and explosives
  • Counterfeit goods and intellectual property violations
  • Undeclared currency over ¤10,000
  • Prohibited wildlife and endangered species products
  • Human trafficking and smuggling networks

Inspection & Screening

The CBCA uses risk-based targeting to identify high-risk shipments and passengers. Inspection methods include:

  • X-ray and CT scanning for cargo and baggage
  • Canine detection teams for narcotics and explosives
  • Biometric identification and document verification
  • Trade data analytics and intelligence sharing with international partners

Report Suspicious Activity

The public can report smuggling, customs fraud, or document forgery through our confidential tipline at +854 300 TIPS (8477) or via the online form.

Penalties & Prosecutions

Customs violations carry civil fines up to 3x the duty owed, plus criminal penalties for fraud, smuggling, or trafficking. Seized goods are forfeited and destroyed or auctioned.

For Travelers

Know Before You Go

Essential information for passengers entering or leaving Praya, including duty-free allowances and declaration requirements.

Personal Duty-Free Allowances

Passengers age 18+ may bring the following without paying duty:

  • Personal goods and souvenirs up to ¤200 value
  • 1 liter of alcohol (beer, wine, or spirits)
  • 200 cigarettes or 50 cigars
  • Personal electronics (1 phone, 1 laptop, 1 camera)

Goods exceeding these limits must be declared and are subject to duty based on the excess value.

Declaration Requirements

All passengers must declare:

  • Cash or monetary instruments over ¤10,000
  • Commercial goods intended for resale
  • Prohibited or restricted items (firearms, medications, food products)
  • Goods acquired abroad exceeding the duty-free allowance

Digital Declaration Form

Complete your customs declaration online before arrival to speed up processing. QR code confirmations are scanned at the border.

Restricted Items Requiring Permits

  • Prescription medications (carry original prescription)
  • Fresh food, plants, and seeds (agricultural inspection required)
  • Drones and radio transmitters
  • Cultural artifacts and antiques

Departure Procedures

Departing passengers should allow 2 hours for international flights and 1 hour for regional bus services. Exit immigration includes biometric verification and departure card collection.

Express Lanes
Trusted travelers

Frequent travelers can apply for the Praya Trusted Traveler program, offering expedited immigration and customs processing at all entry points.

Passenger Rights
Fair treatment

All passengers have the right to courteous treatment, clear explanations of inspection procedures, and privacy during searches. File complaints through our passenger rights office.

About CBCA

Mission, Leadership & Contact

Established in 2018 through the Border Security and Trade Facilitation Act, consolidating immigration and customs services.

Our Mission

The Customs and Border Control Agency safeguards Praya's prosperity through secure borders, fair trade practices, and the efficient movement of people and goods.

Organizational Structure

The CBCA is led by Commissioner Elia Voss and operates through three core divisions:

  • Customs Operations: Tariff administration, trade compliance, and revenue collection
  • Immigration Services: Visa processing, citizenship, and entry/exit control
  • Border Enforcement: Contraband interdiction, investigations, and intelligence

Entry Points Under CBCA Control

As of February 23, 2025, the CBCA assumed control of all entry-exit points:

  • Praya International Airport (air cargo and passengers)
  • Braemar Bus Terminal (regional land border)
  • Port of South Praya (commercial maritime)
  • Port of Braemar (commercial and ferry services)
  • Port of Point Robson (fishing and recreational maritime)

Transparency & Accountability

The CBCA publishes quarterly reports on duty collections, enforcement actions, and processing times. An independent ombudsman reviews traveler and importer complaints.
