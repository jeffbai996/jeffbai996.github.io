Our Mission

Establishing a legal, equitable, and free cannabis market

Statutory Mandate

Since the enactment of the Cannabis Taxation and Regulation Act (R.S. 7 § LC-3396) in 2015, the Government has been committed to building a safe, transparent, and sustainable adult-use cannabis market.

Our Goal

To establish a legal, strong, equitable, and free market for cannabis and cannabis products for responsible adults above the age of 18. We aim to protect both the consumer and the producer with rational, sensible legislation, and to reduce illegal use and illegal markets with favorable taxation and sensible policymaking.

Core Functions

  • Legislation: Developing and refining cannabis regulations to ensure a balanced, fair market
  • Education: Informing consumers and producers about their rights and responsibilities
  • Taxation: Administering the cannabis tax regime under LC-3396
  • Licensing: Issuing and managing commercial cannabis licenses
  • Oversight: Ensuring compliance and protecting legal users aged 18 and over

Legal Framework

  • Cannabis Taxation and Regulation Act (R.S. 7 § LC-3396) – Establishes taxation and regulatory framework (2015)
  • Bill LC3395 – Personal use and cultivation rights
  • Medical Cannabis Access Act – Governs the separate MCAP medical system
  • Cannabis Testing Standards Regulation (2016) – Laboratory and quality standards

Note: Medical Cannabis

The CTB is not responsible for the medical cannabis system. For inquiries about Praya's MCAP system, please visit the appropriate department page on GOV.PRAYA.

Mission Pillars

  • Safety & Health: Every legal product is tested, tracked, and labeled to protect consumers and communities.
  • Equity & Access: Policies ensure responsible adults can participate in the market while marginalized operators have a clear path to licensing.
  • Transparency & Trust: Taxes, licensing, and enforcement are published openly to sustain confidence in the legal system.
  • Innovation & Research: Partnerships with academic institutions advance science-based policy and product standards.

Mission Statement

Guided by the Inland Revenue Department, the Cannabis Tax Bureau builds and defends a legal, strong, equitable, and free cannabis market for adults over 18—grounded in sensible taxation, responsible licensing, and rigorous consumer protections.

History

The Cannabis Tax Bureau was established in March 2015 following the passage of the Cannabis Taxation and Regulation Act. Under the leadership of the Inland Revenue Department, the CTB has overseen the growth of a regulated cannabis industry that prioritizes public safety, consumer protection, and economic opportunity.

Since legalization, Praya has seen a significant reduction in illicit cannabis markets, with tax revenues supporting public health initiatives, education programs, and community development projects.

Commercial Licensing

Licenses for cultivation, processing, retail, and testing operations

Personal Use (No License Required)

Pursuant to Bill LC3395, individuals in Praya are allowed to consume cannabis freely, subject to the following conditions:

  • Must be 18 years of age or older
  • No smoking signs must be obeyed
  • It is illegal to smoke within 5 metres of the doorway to a public building
  • Cultivation of up to 6 plants for personal use is permitted
  • No more than 3 mature plants at any one time

Commercial License Categories

All commercial cannabis activities require licensing from the Cannabis Tax Bureau. Select a license type below to learn more and access the application form.

Cannabis Cultivation License

CTBF-646

Authorizes the commercial cultivation of C. sativa and C. indica species. Required for all production operations regardless of scale.

Processing License

CTBF-647

Required for trimming raw cannabis, producing concentrates, manufacturing edibles, and other value-added processing activities.

Retail Dispensary License

CTBF-648

Authorizes retail sale of cannabis products to consumers aged 18 and older. Includes requirements for security, record-keeping, and age verification.

Testing Laboratory License

CTBF-649

Required for laboratories performing potency testing, contaminant screening, and quality assurance for commercial cannabis products.

Application Requirements

All license applications require the following:

  • Completed application form with applicable fee
  • Proof of Praya citizenship or permanent residency
  • Background check authorization
  • Business registration documents
  • Premises floor plan and security plan
  • Proof of local zoning compliance

Processing Time

Standard applications are processed within 45–60 business days. Expedited processing (30 days) is available for an additional fee.

Application Readiness

  • Entity credentials: Include your PRY business number and primary contact for compliance notices.
  • Zoning confirmation: Upload municipal or county authorization for the proposed site.
  • Security and safety plan: Summarize surveillance, access control, and secure storage measures.
  • Financial disclosures: Provide ownership structure and capital sources for all principals.
  • Community engagement: Outline how your operation supports local jobs and equitable participation.

Quarterly Intake Windows

Complete submissions received by March 15, June 15, September 15, and December 15 enter the next review cycle. Incomplete applications roll to the following window once all required materials are submitted.

Schedule of Taxation

Compliant with Bill LC-3396(2) – Cannabis Tax Act, 2nd rev.

The following taxation schedules have been in effect since March 6, 2015:

Production Tax (LC-3396(2) Cp. 2)

Cannabis production refers to the cultivation of the species C. sativa and C. indica for commercial purposes. Tax is calculated on the wholesale value of harvested product.

Business Type Rate
Production only (no processing or sales)6.4%
Production + Processing (no sales)5.2%
Vertically Integrated (produce, process, sell)4.0%

Processing Tax (LC-3396(2) Cp. 3)

Cannabis processing refers to:

  • Trimming of raw cannabis flowers
  • Production of cannabis concentrates
  • Production of edible cannabis products
  • Manufacturing of topical products

Processing Tax Rate

The processing of cannabis is taxed at 2.5% of the intended sale price of the product (excluding sales tax).

Sales Tax (LC-3396(2) Cp. 4)

Sale refers to selling cannabis in exchange for monetary compensation at a licensed retail outlet.

Sales Tax Rate

The sale of cannabis is taxed at a flat rate of 8.25%.

Tax Filing Requirements

  • Monthly returns: Due by the 15th of the following month
  • Annual reconciliation: Due by March 31
  • Payment: Electronic payment required for amounts over ¤500

Example Tax Calculation

A vertically integrated operator selling a ¤100 product would pay a 4.0% production tax (¤4.00), a 2.5% processing tax (¤2.50), and collect an 8.25% sales tax (¤8.25) from the customer at checkout.

Late Filing Penalties

Late returns incur a penalty of 5% of tax owed plus 1% per month (maximum 25%). Interest accrues at the prime rate plus 4%.

Research Program

University of Praya cannabis research initiative

Research Facility

Research-grade cannabis is cultivated with permission by the University of Praya on a 2,400 square metre hydroponic research facility located in unorganized Francis County.

The facility supports a wide range of research activities including:

  • Cannabinoid pharmacology and therapeutic applications
  • Agricultural science and cultivation optimization
  • Product safety and quality assurance methods
  • Social and public health impact studies

For Researchers

Researchers seeking to obtain samples are asked to contact the University of Praya directly. CTB licenses may be obtained once permission has been granted by the University.

Research License Process

  • Step 1: Contact the University of Praya research department
  • Step 2: Submit research proposal for review
  • Step 3: Obtain written permission from the University
  • Step 4: Submit CTB research license application with University approval
  • Step 5: Receive CTB license and coordinate sample acquisition

Ethics & Data Access

Projects that involve human subjects or data collection must include an institutional ethics approval. Research findings that inform public policy should be shared with CTB within 90 days of publication to support evidence-based regulation.

Current Research Areas

The University of Praya is currently conducting research in the following areas:

  • CBD efficacy in chronic pain management
  • Cannabis-drug interactions
  • Sustainable cultivation practices
  • Standardization of potency testing methods

Consumer Information

Know your rights and responsibilities

Your Rights Under Bill LC3395

  • Adults 18 and over may purchase, possess, and consume cannabis
  • You may cultivate up to 6 plants for personal use (max 3 mature at any time)
  • Consumption is permitted freely, subject to public space restrictions
  • You have the right to purchase tested, labeled products from licensed dispensaries

Where You Cannot Consume

  • Within 5 metres of any public building doorway
  • Anywhere a "No Smoking" sign is posted
  • Schools, playgrounds, and youth facilities
  • Public transit vehicles and stations
  • Other locations as prohibited by local ordinances

Important

Always obey posted signage. Consumption restrictions exist to protect public health and ensure respectful use of shared spaces.

Buying Legal Cannabis

Only purchase cannabis from licensed retail dispensaries. Licensed operators are required to:

  • Verify your age with government-issued ID
  • Provide tested, regulated products with accurate labeling
  • Display their license prominently
  • Issue receipts for all purchases

Transport & Travel

Keep cannabis in sealed, labeled packaging when traveling between licensed sites and your residence. Do not transport cannabis across national borders or outside Praya jurisdiction.

Product Safety

All legal cannabis products in Praya must be tested for:

  • Potency (THC/CBD content)
  • Pesticides and herbicides
  • Heavy metals
  • Microbial contaminants
  • Residual solvents (for concentrates)

Medical Cannabis

For information about medical cannabis access under the Medical Cannabis Access Act, please visit the MCAP pages on GOV.PRAYA. The CTB does not administer the medical cannabis program.

Compliance Guide

Requirements for licensed cannabis businesses

Record Keeping

All licensees must maintain accurate records of:

  • All cannabis received, processed, and sold
  • Inventory counts (daily for retail, weekly for cultivation/processing)
  • Employee records and training certifications
  • Security incident reports
  • Waste disposal logs

Records must be retained for a minimum of 7 years and made available to CTB inspectors upon request.

Security Requirements

  • 24/7 video surveillance of all cannabis handling areas
  • Alarm systems connected to a monitoring service
  • Restricted access to cannabis storage areas
  • Visitor logs for all non-employees

Reporting Requirements

Inspections

CTB inspectors may conduct announced or unannounced inspections during business hours. Licensees must provide access to:

  • All areas of the licensed premises
  • Business records and documentation
  • Security footage
  • Product samples for testing
