Driver & Vehicle Licensing

Get licensed to drive and register your vehicle in Praya

Driver Licensing

To drive legally in the Republic of Praya, you must hold a valid Prayan driver's license. We issue licenses for various vehicle categories based on your qualifications and testing.

License Categories

  • Category A: Motorcycles and scooters (engine > 50cc)
  • Category B: Passenger vehicles (up to 8 passengers, max 3,500 kg)
  • Category C: Heavy vehicles (> 3,500 kg)
  • Category D: Passenger buses (> 8 passengers)
  • Category E: Vehicles with trailers

License Validity

Driver licenses are valid for 10 years for drivers aged 18-60, and 5 years for drivers over 60. Renewal is required before expiration.

Vehicle Registration

All motor vehicles operated on Prayan roads must be registered with the Transport Department. Registration includes:

  • Vehicle identification number (VIN) verification
  • Safety and emissions inspection
  • Insurance verification
  • License plate issuance
  • Annual registration fee payment

Registration Fees (2025)

  • Motorcycles: ₱150 annually
  • Passenger vehicles: ₱300 annually
  • Commercial vehicles: ₱500 annually
  • Heavy vehicles: ₱800 annually

Penalty for Unregistered Vehicles

Operating an unregistered vehicle carries a fine of ₱2,000 and potential vehicle impoundment. Ensure your registration is current.

National Highway Network

Connecting the Republic of Praya from coast to coast

National Highway 9 (NH9)

NH9 is Praya's primary north-south corridor, spanning 412 kilometers from North Port in the north to South Bay in the south. This vital artery connects major population centers and serves as the backbone of our national transport system.

Praya Ring Road

The 78-kilometer Praya Ring Road encircles the capital, providing efficient circumferential movement around Praya City. The Ring Road connects all major highways entering the capital and reduces downtown traffic congestion by 40%.

National Highway 19 (NH19)

NH19 runs 328 kilometers east-west, linking West Point to East Port. Recently expanded with two additional lanes in each direction, NH19 is a key commercial route facilitating trade between coastal regions.

Highway Services

All national highways feature rest areas every 50 km, emergency phones every 2 km, and 24/7 patrol services for breakdowns and accidents.

Airport Expressway

This 34-kilometer expressway provides direct access from Praya City center to Praya International Airport. The limited-access highway features a dedicated bus lane and achieves an average travel time of just 22 minutes.

National Highway 3 (NH3)

The scenic 395-kilometer NH3 follows Praya's eastern coastline, connecting resort towns and fishing villages. Popular with tourists, NH3 offers breathtaking ocean views and access to national beaches and parks.

Winter Travel Advisory

Sections of NH9 and NH3 may experience winter closures due to snow. Check road conditions before traveling during December through February.

Road Safety

Keeping Prayan roads safe for everyone

Traffic Safety Standards

The Transport Department is committed to maintaining the highest road safety standards across the Republic of Praya. Our comprehensive approach includes infrastructure improvements, enforcement, and public education.

Speed Limits

  • Urban areas: 50 km/h
  • Suburban areas: 80 km/h
  • National highways: 110 km/h
  • Expressways: 130 km/h
  • School zones: 30 km/h (during school hours)

Safety Record

Praya has achieved a 35% reduction in traffic fatalities over the past five years through enhanced enforcement and infrastructure improvements.

Mandatory Safety Requirements

  • Seat belts required for all passengers
  • Child safety seats for children under 4 years
  • Helmets required for all motorcycle riders and passengers
  • Headlights required between sunset and sunrise
  • No mobile phone use while driving (hands-free permitted)

Penalties for Violations

  • Speeding (< 20 km/h over): ₱500 fine
  • Speeding (> 20 km/h over): ₱1,500 fine + 3 points
  • Running red light: ₱800 fine + 3 points
  • Driving under influence: ₱5,000 fine + license suspension
  • No seat belt: ₱300 fine

Points System

Accumulating 12 or more points within 2 years results in license suspension. Points remain on your record for 2 years from the date of violation.

Special Transport Permits

Permits for oversized loads and special vehicles

Oversize/Overweight Permits

Vehicles or loads exceeding standard legal limits require special permits from the Transport Department. We evaluate each application based on safety, infrastructure impact, and routing considerations.

Standard Legal Limits

  • Maximum width: 2.5 meters
  • Maximum height: 4.2 meters
  • Maximum length: 12 meters (single vehicle), 18 meters (combination)
  • Maximum weight: 40 tonnes gross vehicle weight

Permit Processing Time

Standard oversize permits are processed within 3-5 business days. Rush processing (24 hours) is available for an additional fee of ₱500.

Commercial Transport Operator License

Businesses operating commercial passenger or freight services require a Transport Operator License (TOL). Requirements include:

  • Business registration with Revenue Department
  • Fleet safety inspection and certification
  • Driver qualification verification
  • Commercial vehicle insurance
  • Annual license fee: ₱2,500

Temporary Import Permits

Visitors bringing vehicles into Praya for temporary use (up to 6 months) must obtain a temporary import permit. Valid foreign driver's licenses are recognized for up to 90 days.

Escort Requirements

Loads exceeding 4 meters width or 50 tonnes weight require police escort. Applicants are responsible for coordination and fees.
