Council Members

Your Representatives

The 82 members of the Legislative Council represent districts across Praya, bringing diverse perspectives to the legislative process.

Leadership

Speaker: Sasha Uri
The Speaker presides over Council sessions, maintains order during debates, recognizes members to speak, and interprets procedural rules. The Speaker is elected by Council members at the start of each term.

Council Structure

The 82-member Council is organized into standing committees that review legislation before it reaches the full floor. Major committees include:

  • Budget & Finance Committee
  • Infrastructure & Transportation Committee
  • Public Safety & Justice Committee
  • Economic Development Committee
  • Health & Human Services Committee
  • Environmental Affairs Committee

Sessions & Schedule

Watch Democracy in Action

Attend Council sessions, watch committee hearings, and participate in the legislative process through public comment and testimony.

Council Sessions

The Legislative Council meets in regular session throughout the year, typically convening on Tuesdays, Wednesdays, and Thursdays. Session days begin at 10:00 AM with the Speaker's call to order.

Order of Business

  • Call to Order: Speaker opens session and reviews agenda
  • Committee Reports: Chairs present bills ready for floor consideration
  • First Reading: New bills introduced and assigned to committee
  • Floor Debate: Members debate bills scheduled for vote
  • Voting: Roll call votes on bills and resolutions
  • Announcements: Committee meetings and upcoming hearings

Live Streaming

All Council sessions and major committee hearings are broadcast live via the Council's website and public television. Archived recordings are available within 24 hours of adjournment.

Attending in Person

The Council chamber public gallery is open during all sessions on a first-come, first-served basis. Visitors must pass through security screening and silence electronic devices.

Committee Meetings

Standing committees meet weekly to review assigned legislation, hear expert testimony, and prepare bills for floor votes. Committee meetings are open to the public with time allocated for citizen comment.

Public Hearings

Major legislation triggers mandatory public hearings where citizens can testify in person. Hearing schedules are posted at least 10 days in advance, and written testimony can be submitted online.

About the Council

The Legislative Branch

Learn about the history, powers, and procedures of Praya's unicameral legislature.

Constitutional Authority

The Legislative Council derives its authority from the Constitution of the Republic of Praya. As the unicameral legislative body, the Council holds the sole power to enact laws, approve the national budget, and provide oversight of the executive branch.

Core Powers

  • Legislative Power: Draft, debate, and enact all national laws
  • Budgetary Authority: Approve annual budgets and authorize spending
  • Executive Oversight: Confirm presidential appointments and investigate executive actions
  • Treaty Ratification: Approve international treaties negotiated by the President
  • Impeachment: Bring charges against executive and judicial officials for misconduct

Legislative Process

Bills may be introduced by any Council member. After introduction, bills are assigned to the appropriate committee for review, public hearings, and markup. If a committee votes to advance a bill, it proceeds to the full Council for floor debate and voting. A simple majority (42 of 82 votes) is required for passage.

Once passed by the Council, bills are sent to the President for signature. The President may sign the bill into law, veto it, or allow it to become law without signature after 10 days. The Council may override a presidential veto with a two-thirds supermajority (55 votes).

History

The current 82-member unicameral structure was established in Praya's founding constitution. The Council has grown from its original 45 members as new districts were added to accommodate population growth.

Council Oath

Each member takes an oath to "faithfully discharge the duties of the office and uphold the Constitution of the Republic of Praya" upon taking office.

Transparency & Accountability

All Council votes, committee proceedings, and financial disclosures are public records. Members must file annual financial disclosure statements and may not vote on matters where they have a personal financial interest.
